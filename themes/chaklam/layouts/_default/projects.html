Culture & Research Philosophy

We are an interdisciplinary team working on the fundamental models of Computer Vision, Natural Language Processing (NLP), and Multimodal Learning. Our philosophy is simple:

  • Rigorous & Reproducible: We don't just chase state-of-the-art numbers. We conduct experiments that explain why models behave the way they do.
  • Efficient & Scalable: We focus on algorithms that are computationally efficient, making high-level AI accessible on standard hardware.

Lab Entry

We welcome students who are curious and ready for hard work. We target top-tier conferences such as NeurIPS, ICLR, CVPR, ACL, and ACM CHI.

  • Robust & Fair Biometrics (Vision): We move beyond standard facial recognition to solve fundamental issues in robustness. Key thesis topics include Adversarial Defense (protecting against physical attacks), Liveness Detection, and Fairness (ensuring models work equally well across all demographics and lighting conditions).
  • Document Intelligence (Vision + Language): Standard OCR is solved, but understanding complex documents is not. We research Multimodal Layout Understanding—teaching models to read charts, tables, and unstructured layouts by combining visual features with textual semantics.
  • Efficient Machine Learning (SLMs): While the world focuses on massive models, we focus on efficiency. We research Small Language Models (SLMs), quantization, and knowledge distillation. The goal is to retain the reasoning capabilities of LLMs while drastically reducing compute costs for edge devices.
  • Speech Representation Learning (Audio): We aim to improve speech understanding without relying on massive labeled datasets. Research focus includes Self-Supervised Learning to handle diverse accents, code-switching (mixing languages), and noisy environments.
  • Generative Audio & Safety (Audio): We work on Controllable Synthesis—generating speech with specific emotions and prosody. Parallel to this, we research AI Safety, specifically developing methods to detect deepfakes and synthesized voice attacks.
  • Multimodal Affective Computing (HCI): We research how to model human internal states by combining voice and text. Instead of simple sentiment analysis, we look at Context-Aware Emotion Recognition to better understand stress, cognitive load, and mental health indicators.
